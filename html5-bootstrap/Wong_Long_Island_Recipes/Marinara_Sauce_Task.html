Marinara Sauce

This marinara is quick, easy, and tastes ten times better than any store-bought sauce. The key to making this sauce authentically Long Island is the ⅓ cup of oil and slow simmer, making it thick and filling. It's hearty, rich with flavor, and pairs perfectly with any Italian-American dish.

Ingredients

Yield: 6-8 servings

Total Cook Time: 40 minutes

  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 5 cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 3 teaspoons salt
  • 3 teaspoons freshly ground pepper
  • 2 (6-ounce) cans of tomato paste
  • 3 (14.5-ounce) cans of diced, unsalted tomatoes
  • 3 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 3 teaspoons dried parsley
  • 3 teaspoons dried thyme
  1. Finely chop the onions and garlic.
  2. Pour ⅓ cup of olive oil into a large pot. Add onions, garlic, salt, and pepper, mix and then cook over medium heat for 5 minutes or until soft.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, diced tomatoes, dried oregano, parsley, and thyme over medium heat.
  4. Boil the sauce over high heat.
    1. Add 1-1.5 cups of water if the sauce looks thick like batter.
  5. Once the sauce starts boiling, simmer under low heat until the sauce has thickened, about 20-25 minutes. Serve and enjoy!
